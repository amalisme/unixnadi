How to Update VMware vCenter Using the CD-ROM (Offline) Method

January 26, 2026 6 min read

If your vCenter Server doesn't have internet access to Broadcom's update repository or you simply prefer to have more control over the update process, the CD-ROM method is your best option. Instead of pulling updates directly from Broadcom's servers, you download the ISO file manually, upload it to a datastore, and mount it to the vCenter appliance.

This offline approach is common in air-gapped environments or when you want to stage updates locally before applying them. Here's how to do it step by step.

Pre-Requisites

Download the ISO from Broadcom Portal

First, log into the Broadcom Support Portal and download the vCenter Server update ISO for your target version. Make sure you're downloading the correct ISO that matches your upgrade path.

Upload the ISO to a Datastore

Once downloaded, upload the ISO file to a datastore accessible by the ESXi host where your vCenter VM is running. You can do this through the vSphere Client or directly via the ESXi Host Client if vCenter is temporarily unavailable.

Create a Fresh Snapshot

Before making any changes, take a snapshot of the vCenter VM. This is your rollback plan if anything goes wrong during the update.

Attach the ISO to vCenter VM

Edit the vCenter VM settings and connect the CD/DVD drive. Point it to the ISO file you uploaded to the datastore. Make sure the "Connected" and "Connect at power on" options are checked.

Update Process

Step 1: Access VAMI

Open your browser and navigate to the vCenter VAMI interface at https://<your-vcenter>:5480. Log in using the root account.

Step 2: Start the Update

Go to the Update tab and click Check Updates. Select Check CD-ROM + URL from the options. The system will detect the mounted ISO and display the available update version.

Step 3: Run Pre-Check

Before proceeding, click on the pre-check option. The system will analyze your current environment and show you the current version, target version, and any potential issues.

Step 4: Review Warnings

After the pre-check completes, you may see warnings about your environment. In my case, the system returned three warnings with suggested resolutions.

You have two options here: resolve the warnings before continuing, or proceed anyway if you've determined they're not critical for your environment. Review each warning carefully. Some may be informational while others could impact your update.

Step 5: Stage the Update

Click Stage and Install to begin. A popup window will appear guiding you through the process.

First, you'll need to accept the End User License Agreement (EULA). After accepting, the system automatically runs the pre-check again. If you didn't resolve the warnings earlier, they'll appear again. But this time with an additional Ignore and Continue button.

If you've assessed the warnings and determined it's safe to proceed, click Ignore and Continue.

Step 6: Confirm Backup

The next step asks you to confirm that you have a backup. Tick the checkbox for "I have backed up vCenter Server and its associated databases". The screen will also show you the estimated time for the update to complete.

Click Finish to start the update.

Step 7: Wait for Update to Complete

The update process begins. During this time, vCenter will be completely unavailable. You won't be able to access the vSphere Client or perform any management tasks. This is normal.

Don't interrupt the process. Don't power off the VM. Just wait for it to complete. The time varies depending on your environment, but the estimate shown earlier should be fairly accurate.

Post-Update Verification

Once the update finishes, wait 2-5 minutes for all services to fully start before attempting to log in.

Verify in VAMI

Log back into VAMI at https://<your-vcenter>:5480 and check the version number displayed. Also review the health status to ensure all services are running properly.

Verify in vSphere Client

Log into the vSphere Client and navigate to Help ‚Üí About to confirm the vCenter and vSphere versions match your target update.

Test Basic Operations

Don't just assume everything works, test it. Perform some basic administrative tasks to confirm vCenter is functioning normally:

  • vMotion a VM between hosts
  • Create a new snapshot on any VM or appliance
  • Browse datastores
  • Check that all ESXi hosts are connected and healthy

Monitor and Clean Up

Keep an eye on your vCenter for about a week after the update. Watch for any unexpected behavior, performance issues, or errors in the logs. If everything looks stable after a week, you can safely delete the pre-update snapshot you created earlier.

Wrapping Up

The CD-ROM method takes a bit more preparation than the online URL method, but it gives you complete control over the update process. It's especially useful when your vCenter can't reach Broadcom's repository, or when you need to update multiple vCenter instances using the same ISO.

The key steps to remember: download the ISO, upload to datastore, snapshot/manual backup before updating, attach the ISO, and verify everything works after the update completes. And don't forget to delete that snapshot after a week of stable operation.
